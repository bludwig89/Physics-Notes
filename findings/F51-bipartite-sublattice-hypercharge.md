## 1. Claim

The body-centered-cubic Weyl walk of Papers 1–2 is **bipartite**: every nearest-neighbour hop flips a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ sublattice parity $P$. The one-parameter group $U_Y(\theta) = e^{i\theta P}$ generated by that parity is

1. an **abelian** internal symmetry,
2. **conserved** by the physical (two-tick, stroboscopic) dynamics,
3. **commuting** with the cell spin/isospin $SU(2)$ (Schur scalar on each spin irrep), and
4. **orthogonal to chirality** (defined identically within each branch, never mixing $\pm$).

Properties (1)–(4) are exactly the defining properties of the hypercharge factor $U(1)_Y$ in $SU(2)\times U(1)_Y$. Moreover, by internal-dimension counting the $s=2$ BCC cell has **no room** for an independent internal $U(1)$: the only abelian charge available to extend $SU(2)_L \to SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y$ without enlarging the cell Hilbert space is this sublattice charge. Hence — at the level of *which* degree of freedom seats hypercharge — the bipartite sublattice is forced.

## 2. The BCC walk is bipartite (grounded facts)

From `ca_bcc.bcc_unitary` / Paper 1 Eq. 15, the single-tick unitary on the 2-spinor is

$$A^{s}(\mathbf k) \;=\; u^{s}(\mathbf k)\,I \;-\; i\,\boldsymbol\sigma\!\cdot\!\mathbf n^{s}(\mathbf k),\qquad s=\pm,$$

with $c_i \equiv \cos(k_i/\sqrt3)$, $s_i \equiv \sin(k_i/\sqrt3)$ and

$$u^{s} = c_xc_yc_z + s\,s_xs_ys_z,\quad
\mathbf n^{s} = \begin{pmatrix} s_xc_yc_z - s\,c_xs_ys_z \\ -s\,c_xs_yc_z + s_xc_ys_z \\ c_xc_ys_z + s\,s_xs_yc_z \end{pmatrix}.$$

Two structural facts about this operator:

- **No on-site term.** Paper 2 proves $A_0 = 0$: the walk is a *pure hop* to the 8 body-diagonal neighbours $(\pm1,\pm1,\pm1)$, with no self-loop.
- **Every entry is a product of exactly three trig factors**, one per axis ($u$ and each $n_a$ are sums of terms $c\,c\,c$ or $s\,s\,s$ — always three factors).

Work in the natural lattice coordinates $q_i = k_i/\sqrt3 \in (-\pi,\pi]$, so the hops carry phases $e^{i(\pm q_x\pm q_y\pm q_z)}$ on a unit-spaced $\mathbb{Z}^3$. Each body-diagonal hop changes the coordinate sum $x_1+x_2+x_3$ by $\pm1\pm1\pm1 \in\{\pm1,\pm3\}$ — **always odd**. Define the sublattice (staggering) parity

$$P \;=\; (-1)^{x_1+x_2+x_3} \;=\; e^{i\mathbf Q\cdot\mathbf x},\qquad \mathbf Q = (\pi,\pi,\pi)\ \text{in } q\ \big(= (\pi\sqrt3,\pi\sqrt3,\pi\sqrt3)\ \text{in } k\big).$$

$P$ two-colours $\mathbb{Z}^3$ into the even/odd sublattices (the corner + body-centre sublattices of [F49](F49-bcc-finite-k-weinberg-angle.md) §Step 2), and because every hop is odd, the walk graph is bipartite.

### 2.1 The single-tick walk anticommutes with $P$

Shifting $q_i \to q_i + \pi$ sends every $c_i \to -c_i$ and $s_i \to -s_i$, so each three-factor product flips sign:

$$A^{s}(\mathbf q + \mathbf Q) \;=\; -\,A^{s}(\mathbf q)\qquad(\text{both branches}).$$

In real space this is $P\,W\,P^{-1} = -W$, i.e. $\{P, W\} = 0$. **This is the algebraic statement of bipartiteness.** (Test **S1**, residual $1.1\times10^{-15}$.)

## 3. From the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ to a conserved $U(1)$

A bare anticommuting $\mathbb{Z}_2$ is not yet a charge — under one tick a definite-sublattice state is carried to the *other* sublattice. The resolution is that the physical fermion lives on the **stroboscopic (two-tick) lattice**, on which $P$ is conserved:

$$W^2(\mathbf q+\mathbf Q) = \big(-W(\mathbf q)\big)^2 = W^2(\mathbf q)\;\;\Longrightarrow\;\;[\,W^2,\,P\,]=0.$$

(Test **S2**, residual $1.8\times10^{-15}$.) Because $W^2$ is block-diagonal in the sublattice label and $P$ is constant ($\pm1$) on each block, the full one-parameter group

$$U_Y(\theta) \;=\; e^{i\theta P} \;=\; \cos\theta\,I + i\sin\theta\,P,\qquad U_Y(\theta)\,e_{\rm even}=e^{+i\theta},\ U_Y(\theta)\,e_{\rm odd}=e^{-i\theta},$$

commutes with the stroboscopic dynamics. So the sublattice label is a **conserved abelian charge** $q_P=\pm1$ of the physical-time evolution — property (2). This is precisely the staggered-$U(1)$ that [F49](F49-bcc-finite-k-weinberg-angle.md) §Step 2 invoked but did not derive.

---

## 4. Why it is the hypercharge slot, not part of $SU(2)$

### 4.1 $P$ is a scalar on the spin index (commutes with $SU(2)$)

$P = e^{i\mathbf Q\cdot\mathbf x}\otimes I_2$ acts only on the position factor; on the internal $\mathbb{C}^2$ it is the identity. Hence $[P,\sigma_a]=0$ for $a=x,y,z$ (Test **S4**, residual exactly $0$). By **Schur's lemma**, an operator commuting with the full $SU(2)$ acting irreducibly on $\mathbb{C}^2$ is a scalar on that irrep — so $U_Y(\theta)$ cannot rotate spin/isospin states into one another; it can only assign each multiplet a phase. That is the structural signature of an abelian factor that *commutes with* $SU(2)$ rather than living inside it — exactly the relation $U(1)_Y$ bears to $SU(2)_L$. The same conclusion transfers to the per-cell $SU(2)_L$ built on the branch/isospin index (F34/F35) and to the F41 gauge field $U(x)$: all act on the internal index, on which $P$ is the identity, so $[P, SU(2)_L]=0$ and $[P,U(x)\,\text{(SU(2) part)}]=0$.

### 4.2 The sublattice $U(1)$ is orthogonal to chirality

The chirality involution is the *distinct* map $\mathbf k\to-\mathbf k$, which exchanges the two branches ($\omega_+(-\mathbf k)=\omega_-(\mathbf k)$, `ca_bcc`). The staggering $\mathbf q\to\mathbf q+\mathbf Q$ is diagonal in the branch label — §2.1 holds *separately* for $s=+$ and $s=-$ with independent residuals $7.9\times10^{-16}$ and $8.5\times10^{-16}$ (Test **S3**). So $U_Y$ commutes with the chirality label: the sublattice charge is orthogonal to handedness — property (4), as required of a $U(1)_Y$ that assigns hypercharge across both chiralities of a Dirac field.

### 4.3 Dimension counting: there is no other abelian slot

Papers 1–2 fix the BCC cell internal dimension at the minimum $s=2$. That $\mathbb{C}^2$ is **entirely consumed** by the spin $SU(2)$ (its generators $\sigma_a$ span all traceless Hermitian operators on $\mathbb{C}^2$; the only $SU(2)$-commuting on-cell operator is the identity, i.e. a *trivial* phase that acts equally on everything and therefore cannot distinguish multiplets). A non-trivial internal abelian charge would require enlarging the cell — which the minimality axiom forbids. The **only** non-trivial abelian quantum number available without enlarging the cell is a property of the *graph*: the bipartite sublattice parity. Therefore any Higgs-free hypercharge (F41) must be carried by precisely this generator; no other DOF of the minimal BCC walk can carry it.

This is the representation-theory content of the claim: not merely that the sublattice *can* carry $U(1)_Y$, but that — given $s=2$ minimality — it is the **unique** seat for an abelian charge commuting with the walk's $SU(2)$.

## 5. Connection to the existing hypercharge construction (F41/F38)

[F41](F41-hypercharge-higgs-free-su2.md) extends the F27 pure-gauge field $U(x)$ to carry the Higgs-equivalent $U(1)_Y$ phase $D(\alpha)=\mathrm{diag}(e^{+i\alpha\Delta Y_\nu/2}, e^{+i\alpha\Delta Y_e/2})$, with the abelian phase required to *commute with* $SU(2)_L$. F51 supplies the missing structural answer to **where that abelian phase lives**: it is the stroboscopically-conserved sublattice $U(1)$ of §3, which §4.1 shows commutes with $SU(2)_L$ by Schur as an identity, not as an imposed constraint. The F41 Ward identities (Y1–Y4, residuals $\sim10^{-16}$) are then the *gauged* expression of the global sublattice charge derived here.

The **charge spectrum** ($Y_L=-1$, etc.) is fixed independently by anomaly cancellation ([F38](F38-fg1-anomaly-cancellation.md), six exact-rational zeros) and reproduces the measured electric charges through Gell-Mann–Nishijima $Q=T_3+Y/2$ (Test **S5**, exact over $\mathbb{Q}$). F51 does not re-derive those values; it identifies the carrier. The bare sublattice charge is $q_P=\pm1$ — the fractional SM hypercharges arise once colour multiplicity (3 sublattice-correlated copies) and the doublet structure are layered on, which is the F38/F49 bookkeeping, not this finding's.

## 7. What this derives and what it does not

**Derived (this finding):**
- The BCC walk is bipartite: $\{P,W\}=0$ exactly (S1).
- The sublattice parity generates an abelian $U(1)$ that is *conserved* by the physical two-tick dynamics: $[W^2,P]=0$ (S2).
- That $U(1)$ commutes with the cell $SU(2)$ (Schur scalar, S4) and with chirality (S3) — the defining relations of the $U(1)_Y$ factor in $SU(2)\times U(1)_Y$.
- Given $s=2$ minimality, the sublattice is the **unique** carrier of a non-trivial abelian charge commuting with the walk's $SU(2)$ (§4.3).

**Not derived here (separate findings):**
- The hypercharge **values** $Y_i$ — fixed by anomaly cancellation, [F38](F38-fg1-anomaly-cancellation.md).
- The coupling **ratio** $g'^2/g^2 = 2/7 \to \sin^2\theta_W = 2/9$ — [F49](F49-bcc-finite-k-weinberg-angle.md), still partial. F51 closes F49's first "not yet derived" bullet (*why $U(1)_Y$ is the sublattice-staggered $U(1)$*); F49's second bullet (*why $SU(2)_L$ is bond-axis-summed*) and the bare-coupling lattice-action derivation remain open.
- How leptons/quarks distribute across the two sublattices (F49 open follow-up 4) — an anomaly-consistency check, not addressed here.
