## The fork

Two findings give the gravitational coupling opposite light-cone-speed powers:

- **F58-Q3a** (clock-rate channel): the bare $6$-neighbour wave operator has symbol $\widehat{(-\Delta)}(\mathbf k)=4J\sum_i\sin^2(k_i/2)\to S_\text{bare}|\mathbf k|^2$, and its signal speed is $c_\text{lat}=\sqrt{S_\text{bare}}$. So $S_\text{bare}=c_\text{lat}^2$, and reading $1/G\propto S_\text{bare}$ gives $1/G\propto c_\text{lat}^2=1/d$.
- **F59/F56** (Sakharov channel): the graviton kinetic term induced by integrating out the matter modes is $B\equiv\frac{1}{16\pi G}=\eta g_*\!\int_\text{BZ}\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\frac{1}{2\omega}\propto\frac{1}{c_\text{lat}}=\sqrt d$.

The F58 finding wrote "$1/G\propto c_\text{lat}^2\propto\sqrt d$", which is internally inconsistent ($c_\text{lat}^2=1/d\neq\sqrt d$). The fork is real and must be resolved before the $(a,\tau)$ selection power ($d^{+1/4}$ vs $d^{-1/4}$) is trustworthy.

## The resolution — tree vs loop

$S_\text{bare}=c_\text{lat}^2$ is the kinematic identity $\omega^2=c_\text{lat}^2|\mathbf k|^2$ of a **tree-level** wave operator: it is the spatial stiffness a *fundamental* graviton would have, and it equals $c_\text{lat}^2$ by definition, carrying no loop information. $B$ is the **loop-induced** stiffness — the same kinetic term, but generated by the matter vacuum's response rather than put in by hand. The two are different objects and scale oppositely in $c_\text{lat}$; on a common hopping/$c_\text{lat}$ footing:

| object | scaling | residual |
|---|---|---|
| bare tree stiffness $S_\text{bare}$ | $c_\text{lat}^{\,2.0000}$ | lock $S_\text{bare}/c_\text{lat}^2$ spread $1.1\times10^{-16}$ |
| induced loop stiffness $B$ | $c_\text{lat}^{\,-1.0000}$ | $B\,c_\text{lat}=0.08001$ const |
| gap $B/S_\text{bare}$ | $c_\text{lat}^{\,-3.0000}$ | exact $-3$ |

The gap is exactly $c_\text{lat}^3$ ($=d^{-3/2}$) — the universal difference between a tree-level operator stiffness ($\propto c_\text{lat}^2$) and a one-loop induced stiffness over the relativistic dispersion ($\propto 1/c_\text{lat}$, the $\int d^3k/(2\omega)$ phase space). Neither result is wrong; they answer different questions.

## Which one is physical

The project has committed to **emergent gravity**: F52 sources the rest-leg potential from mass with *no* fundamental graviton kinetic term; F57 shows the metric kinetic term is *generated* by the matter back-reaction ("dynamical, not assumed"); F56/F59 compute it as the induced Sakharov coefficient. In that ontology there **is** no tree-level graviton stiffness — the physical kinetic term is the induced $B$. Therefore

$$\frac{1}{16\pi G}=B\propto\frac{1}{c_\text{lat}}=\sqrt d,$$

and **F58-Q3a's $c_\text{lat}^2$ is the stiffness of the bare Poisson *operator* used in the solver, not the physical Newton coupling.** What F58 correctly derives from the neighbour rule is the *form* of the field equation — the $4\pi$ (Q1), the $\nabla^2$ (Q2), and the structural $c_\text{lat}$-lock (Q3a); what F56/F57/F59 supply is the *magnitude* $1/G$, whose $c_\text{lat}$-power comes from the loop, not the bare operator. Form and magnitude come from different places; that is the whole reconciliation.

## Convention-free clincher

A bare operator stiffness is a field-normalization-dependent quantity, not by itself a physical coupling. The induced channel instead computes the **dimensionless, convention-free** ratio $\ell_P/a$ directly (F59: $\ell_P^2/a^2=1/(2\pi\eta g_*\sqrt d)$), which is what enters the Finding-10 selection:

$$\text{induced (physical): }\ a=\sqrt{2\pi\eta g_*}\;d^{+1/4}\,\ell_P\quad(\text{$a=1.347\,\ell_P$ at }d{=}3),$$
$$\text{bare (if mis-read as a coupling): }\ a\sim d^{-1/4}\ell_P\quad(0.778\,\ell_P).$$

The induced channel is the one tied to a dimensionless observable, so the selection power is $d^{+1/4}$ — F59 confirmed, F58's $c_\text{lat}^2$ explained, not contradicted.
