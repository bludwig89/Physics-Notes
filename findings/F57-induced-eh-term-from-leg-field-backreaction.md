## The question

F56 Part B *assumed* the Sakharov induced Einstein–Hilbert term and computed its scaling. F57 asks whether the back-reaction of the matter modes on the F52/F55 leg-fields actually *generates* that term — i.e. exhibits the mechanism rather than positing it.

## Set-up

Integrating out the propagating F26 modes in a slowly-varying background metric gives the one-loop effective action $W[g]=\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}\ln(-\Box_g+\dots)=\int d^4x\sqrt{-g}\,[\Lambda_\text{ind}+R/(16\pi G_\text{ind})+\dots]$. The coefficient of $R$ — the induced $1/(16\pi G)$ — is the gradient response of the matter vacuum to a metric perturbation: the $q^2$ piece of the matter polarization. Coupling the Newtonian potential $\Phi$ (the rest leg, $h_{00}=-2\Phi/c^2$) to the matter density (the F52 coupling $S_\text{int}=\int\Phi\rho$), the vacuum static density polarization is

$$\Pi(q)=\int_\text{BZ}\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\,\frac{1}{\omega(\mathbf k)+\omega(\mathbf k+\mathbf q)},\qquad \omega = \text{F26 BCC dispersion},$$

with small-$q$ expansion $\Pi(q)=\Pi(0)-\Pi_2 q^2+\dots$. In position space $S_\text{eff}[\Phi]=-\tfrac12\int\Phi\,\Pi\,\Phi \supset \tfrac12\Pi_2(\nabla\Phi)^2$ — so $\Pi_2$ is the induced graviton kinetic (EH) term.

## Interpretation — and a correction to F56

1. **The EH term is generated by the back-reaction.** The matter loop produces a positive $(\nabla\Phi)^2$ stiffness ($\Pi_2>0$, K2): a kinetic term for the metric potential appears from integrating out the modes. Gravity becomes dynamical as a consequence of the matter–leg-field coupling, not by fiat. The Sakharov mechanism F56 assumed is thereby exhibited as an explicit, finite lattice integral.

2. **The lattice removes the UV ambiguity.** Because the Brillouin zone is compact, $\Pi$ is UV-finite (K1) — there is no regulator freedom. The coefficient F56 called "scheme-dependent" is, on a physical lattice, a definite number set by the F26 dispersion.

3. **F56 identified the wrong sector.** F56 used $\Pi(0)=\int d^3k/(2\omega)$, which scales as $\Lambda^2$ — but that $q=0$ value is the **vacuum-energy / cosmological-constant** sector, not Newton's constant. The actual EH/Newton coefficient is the gradient response $\Pi_2$, which scales only **logarithmically** (K3). So the rest-leg (density-density) channel reproduces **Adler–Zee running gravity**, not the quadratic Sakharov estimate. F56's $\Lambda^2$ was the right scaling for the wrong object.

## What is reduced vs what remains

- **Reduced (derived):** that the back-reaction generates an EH kinetic term (mechanism exhibited); that it is UV-finite on the lattice (no scheme freedom); that it has the correct sign (stable, attractive gravity); that the rest-leg/Newtonian channel runs logarithmically.
- **Not produced as a pure number:** the absolute $1/G$ still needs (i) the count $g_*$ of gravitating mode polarizations (countable on the BCC but not yet summed here — this is the single-scalar-density channel), (ii) an IR matching scale (the log running means $G$ depends on the scale at which it is defined — physical, not a defect), and (iii) the **spatial-stress (kinetic-leg, F55) channel** of the full $T_{\mu\nu}T_{\alpha\beta}$ correlator, which supplies the quadratic Sakharov piece the density channel alone misses.

This last point ties the result back to the F52/F55 two-leg structure elegantly: the **rest leg (F52) gives the Newtonian/running-$G$ sector**, and the **kinetic leg (F55) is expected to give the quadratic Sakharov sector**. Computing the kinetic-leg channel is the natural next step.

## Caveats

- The polarization here is the scalar-density ($T_{00}T_{00}$) channel — the $h_{00}$/Newtonian sector. The full graviton kinetic term requires the tensor $T_{\mu\nu}T_{\alpha\beta}$ correlator including spatial stress.
- "$\Pi_2$ runs logarithmically" is read from $\Pi_2\propto\Lambda^{0.25}$ over the probed range plus a clean positive $\Pi_2\approx a\ln\Lambda+b$ fit ($a\approx3\times10^{-3}>0$); it is not claimed to machine precision.
- The identification $1/(16\pi G_\text{ind})=\Pi_2$ fixes the Newtonian-sector normalisation; the diff-invariant completion (relating it to the full $R$ coefficient) is assumed, not derived.
